----------------------------------------

Authors: Ze-Min Han, Welera Haissou Elodie, Wen-Juan Zhang, Pei-Chun Xu, Li-Hua Yan, et al. (7 authors)
Journal: Therapeutic drug monitoring
Published: 2026-01-29
PMID: 41606780

SUMMARY:
This multicenter retrospective study of 302 Crohn disease patients found that transmural healing requires significantly higher adalimumab concentrations than mucosal healing (15.0 vs 9.9 mcg/mL). The optimal cutoff concentration for transmural healing was 10.3 mcg/mL compared to 6.4 mcg/mL for mucosal healing.

RELEVANCE RATIONALE:

STUDY DETAILS:
  Study Design: Multicenter retrospective study
  Primary Endpoints: Association between transmural healing/mucosal healing and serum trough levels of adalimumab/ustekinumab
  Notable Results: Adalimumab concentrations for transmural healing vs mucosal healing: 15.0 vs 9.9 mcg/mL (P=0.02); optimal cutoff concentrations: 10.3 vs 6.4 mcg/mL; high drug concentration independently associated with transmural healing (OR 9.90, P<0.01)

Journal: Journal of the European Academy of Dermatology and Venereology : JEADV
Published: 2026-01-24
PMID: 41580305

SUMMARY:
A Delphi consensus of 54 European HS experts established criteria for first-line biologic therapy in hidradenitis suppurativa, moving away from traditional antibiotic-first approaches. STUDY DETAILS:
  Study Design: Delphi consensus study
  Primary Endpoints: Expert consensus levels on 16 statements regarding first-line biologic/small molecule therapy criteria using Likert scale (-5 to +5) with agreement thresholds of ≥70% (majority), ≥75% (consensus), and ≥90% (strong consensus)
  Notable Results: Strong consensus (≥90%) achieved for contraindicated antibiotic patients, rapid progressors, severe disease, syndromic HS, and patients with inflammatory comorbidities.
